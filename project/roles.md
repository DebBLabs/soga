# Canonical Delegate Roles

Status: Draft

Purpose:

Define reusable delegate roles used across SOGA scenarios.

The IIW scenario includes human relationships such as niece, grandson,
lawyer, and business partner.

Those human labels should remain visible in scenarios because they make
the situation understandable.

For architecture and governance evaluation, they should map to reusable
delegate roles.

---

## Subject

Example:

Alice

Meaning:

The principal whose intent, interests, authority, and current state are
being governed.

---

## Family Delegate

Examples:

- Niece
- Grandson
- Daughter
- Son
- Spouse
- Sibling

Typical Authority:

- Personal support
- Daily living assistance
- Limited purchases
- Family coordination

Governance Notes:

Family relationship alone does not define authority.

Authority must still be declared, evidenced, and evaluated against the
mission step.

---

## Caregiver Delegate

Examples:

- Caregiver
- Care coordinator
- Trusted helper
- Facility contact

Typical Authority:

- Care coordination
- Appointment support
- Well-being checks
- Escalation support

Governance Notes:

Caregiver authority may support RESTRICT outcomes where approval or
supervision is required before execution.

---

## Legal Delegate

Examples:

- Lawyer
- Guardian
- Power of Attorney
- Legal representative

Typical Authority:

- Legal representation
- Contract review
- Formal authorization
- Estate or guardianship actions

Governance Notes:

Legal authority may be broad but still mission-bound.

SOGA evaluates whether the authority should be exercised for the current
mission step under current conditions.

---

## Financial Delegate

Examples:

- Financial advisor
- Trustee
- Accountant
- Family member with financial authority

Typical Authority:

- Bill payment
- Account management
- Budget support
- Limited or high-value financial transactions

Governance Notes:

Financial authority often requires thresholds, scope limits, or secondary
approval.

---

## Business Delegate

Examples:

- Business partner
- Executive assistant
- Operations delegate
- Company representative

Typical Authority:

- Business operations
- Commercial decisions
- Contractual coordination
- Vendor or partner actions

Governance Notes:

Business authority may depend on mission purpose, organizational role,
and conflict-of-interest constraints.

---

# Usage Rules

1. Scenario narratives may use human labels such as niece, grandson,
   lawyer, or business partner.

2. Governance documentation should also identify the normalized delegate
   role.

3. Do not assume authority from relationship alone.

4. Always distinguish:

   - human relationship
   - delegate role
   - authority evidence
   - mission step requirement

5. Protocol artifacts provide evidence of authority.

6. SOGA determines whether that authority should still be exercised now.
